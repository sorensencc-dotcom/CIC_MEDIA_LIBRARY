FORTUNE MAGAZINE - March 1944
FORD'S PRODUCTION GENIUS RESIGNS

Charles Sorensen, 62, who transformed Ford Motor
Company's manufacturing operations over four decades,
has submitted his resignation effective immediately.

Sorensen, born in Odense, Denmark in 1881, joined Ford
in 1905 and rose to become the chief production engineer
behind the assembly line and Willow Run bomber plant.

Henry Ford accepted the resignation on March 4, 1944.
Sources close to Harry Bennett's Service Department

suggest the departure was long anticipated.

Sorensen is expected to join Willys-Overland Motors.
